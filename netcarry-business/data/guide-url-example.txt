               <div class="clearer"></div><h2 id="Section_Troubleshooting">Troubleshooting<a class="anchor" href="#Section_Troubleshooting" title="链接到该区域">&nbsp;&para;&nbsp;</a></h2>

<p>Track down a number of hardware problems using the <a href="/Wiki/MacBook_Pro_13%22_Unibody_Troubleshooting">MacBook Pro 13&quot; Unibody Troubleshooting Guide</a>.</p>

<div class="clearer"></div><h2 id="Section_Upgrades">Upgrades<a class="anchor" href="#Section_Upgrades" title="链接到该区域">&nbsp;&para;&nbsp;</a></h2>

<p>There are a number of components in the MacBook Pro 13&quot; Unibody that can be cost effectively upgraded.</p>

<ul><li><strong>Memory:</strong> The MacBook Pro 13&quot; Unibody comes with 2 or 4 GB RAM standard, and accepts a maximum of 8 GB. If your MacBook Pro is still running with only the stock RAM, upgrading will provide a dramatic performance boost. You can <a href="/Guide/MacBook+Pro+13-Inch+Unibody+Mid+2009+RAM+Replacement/1334">upgrade</a> to two <a href="/Store/Mac/PC3-8500-4-GB-RAM-Chip/IF111-036">4 GB</a> chips for a total of 8 GB very easily.</li><li><strong>Hard Drive:</strong> 160 or 250 GB hard drives came standard with the MacBook Pro 13&quot; Unibody. You can easily <a href="/Guide/MacBook+Pro+13-Inch+Unibody+Mid+2009+Hard+Drive+Replacement/1337">upgrade</a> the drive to <a href="/Store/Mac/750-GB-7200-RPM-2-5-Inch-Hard-Drive/IF107-099">750 GB</a>.</li><li><strong>Battery:</strong> The <a href="/Store/Mac/MacBook-Pro-13-Inch-Unibody-Mid-2009-Battery/IF163-003">battery</a> is certainly replaceable, even though Apple doesn't consider it to be &quot;user serviceable.&quot; Unscrew twelve screws, unplug the battery connector, and the battery can be removed!</li></ul>

<div class="clearer"></div><h2 id="Section_Identification_and_Background">Identification and Background<a class="anchor" href="#Section_Identification_and_Background" title="链接到该区域">&nbsp;&para;&nbsp;</a></h2>

<p>The MacBook Pro 13&quot; Unibody is clearly differentiated from other MacBook Pros by its smaller size. However, it does resemble its larger siblings, the <a href="/Topic/Macbook_Pro_15%22_Unibody">MacBook Pro 15&quot; Unibody</a> and the <a href="/Topic/Macbook_Pro_17%22_Unibody">MacBook Pro 17&quot; Unibody</a>.</p>

<p>Use the <a href="/info/ID-your-Mac">laptop identification system</a> to help you identify your machine. MacBook Pros tend to look very similar, and it's important to know which machine you have before ordering any replacement parts.</p>
